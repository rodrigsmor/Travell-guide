Tourism in Peru

Peru is located in the western portion of South America. Its territory, bathed by the Pacific Ocean, is limited to the south by Chile, to the east by Bolivia and Brazil, and to the north by Ecuador and Colombia, also, is the third largest country in South America, after Brazil and Argentina.

Throughout Peru it is possible to find wonderful remnants of the past that amaze travelers from all over the world. Since the 2000s, Tourism in Peru has been the country's third largest industry, behind only fishing and mining. Tourism is focused on archeological monuments, ecotourism in the Peruvian Amazon, cultural tourism in colonial cities, gastronomic tourism, adventure tourism and beach tourism. Tourism is the fastest growing business sector in Peru, growing annually at a rate of 25% over the past five years. Tourism is growing in Peru faster than in any other country in South America.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca site located on a ridge between the Huayna Picchu and Machu Picchu mountains in Peru. It sits 7,970 feet (2,430 meters) above sea level on the eastern slope of the Andes and overlooks the Urubamba River hundreds of feet below.

Location: Cusco Region, Peru

Vinicunca, Rainbow Mountain

Vinicunca, or Winikunka, also called Montaña de Siete Colores, Montaña de Colores or Rainbow Mountain, is a mountain in the Andes of Peru with an altitude of 5,200 metres (17,100 ft) above sea level. It is located on the road to the Ausangate mountain, in the Cusco region, between the districts of Cusipata, province of Quispicanchi, Pitumarca, and the province of Canchis.

Location: Cusco Region, Peru
