About Peru

As one of the member countries of Latin America, Peru is located in the western part of South America, in front of the borders with Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Bolivia and Chile. In its vast territory, with more than 1.2 million km², it covers three regions: Litoral, Serra and Selva. The population today exceeds 31.5 thousand inhabitants.

Peru is one of the most diverse countries in the world, being extremely multicultural, with many traditions, an award-winning cuisine and remembered for its vast natural reserves. It has 12 UNESCO global heritages and donated 84 of the 117 ecosystems in the world.

Due to Spanish colonization, Spanish is the official language of the country. However, in addition to Spanish, 47 native languages ​​can be found, such as Quichua (also known as Quechua) and Aymara, two pre-Columbian indigenous languages.

Peru's history

Peru's history is a history of ancient civilizations, beginning their development over ten millennia in the cold Puna region of the Andes.

Like the rest of the American continent, its history is characterized by a diversity of cultures and peoples who inhabited the territories that are now part of the Republic of Peru, in addition to being marked by European colonization, mainly Spanish.

The region of present-day Peru was inhabited by several peoples, but the main and most recognized one is the Inca civilization, which was conquered in 1532 with the invasion of the Spaniards in the territories of what they called the “New World”.

Tourism

Thanks to the legacy of powerful ancient civilizations, Peru is home to over 5,000 archaeological sites. Many of them are still full of mystery, but they transport the visitor back to the time when these peoples first appeared. For example, a visit to Machu Picchu reveals the perfection of the Inca Empire; It is possible to reach this sacred place aboard luxury trains that pass through beautiful mountain landscapes dotted with colorful Andean villages.
